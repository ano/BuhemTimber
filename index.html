POPULAR PNG TIMBER SPECIES

Papua New Guinea (PNG) is blessed with diverse and valuable timber resources. Below is a list of the most popular timber species found in PNG:

Kwila/Merbau 

(Intsia spp.)

Known for its durability and attractive reddish-brown color, Kwila is widely used in outdoor furniture, decking, and boat building.

Rosewood  

(Pterocarpus indicus)

A highly valued timber species with a rich reddish-brown hue, often used in high-end furniture, musical instruments, and specialty items.

Taun 

(Pometia pinnata)

A versatile timber used in construction, furniture, and cabinetry due to its workability and good strength properties.

Vitex 

(Vitex cofassus)

A hardwood species popular for its stability and resistance to decay, commonly used in boat building, decking, and flooring.

SOME OTHER POPULAR PNG TIMBER SPECIES

Below is a list of some of the other popular timber species found in PNG

  1. Calophyllum (Calophyllum spp.): A hardwood species used of timber often used for furniture, cabinetry, and boat building due to its durability and attractive appearance.
  2. Erima (Octomeles sumatrana): Erima timber is easily worked by hand and machine tools, and dried timber has excellent technical properties. It is generally used in used in various woodworking applications, including construction, boat building, and crafting of furniture and tools. It is valued for its strength and durability, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor use.
  3. Terminalia, Red-Brown (Terminalia spp.): Generally used in construction, furniture, and joinery applications.
  4. Quandong (Elaeocarpus spp.): Generally used for for light interior construction, weatherboard, boat building, aircraft building, furniture, joinery, scantlings, mouldings, boxes, pallets, shuttering, brushware, turnery, oars, match splints and carvings.
  5. PNG OAK (Lithocarpus): PNG Oak is a durable, long-lasting wood. The timber is commonly used in house-building for beams, columns and planks, and occasionally also for furniture and tool handles.
  6. Dillenia (Dillenia papuana): Used locally as a commercial hardwood.
  7. Walnut (Dracontomelondao): A hardwood species used for furniture, cabinetry, and decorative veneers due to its attractive grain patterns.
  8. Terminalia (Terminalia braseii): A hardwood that is moderately lightweight to medium-weight and is generally used in construction, furniture, and joinery applications.
  9. Red Cedar (Juniperus virginiana): A softwood species valued for its aromatic and insect-repellent properties, often used in outdoor applications like fencing, decking, and siding.
  10. Labula (Anthocephaluschinensis): A hardwood species used in construction, interior trimmings, and joinery due to its workability and attractive appearance.
  11. Pencil Cedar (Palaquiun spp.): A hardwood species used for furniture, carving, and musical instruments due to its fine and even texture.
  12. Basswood (Tilia Americana): A softwood often used in carving, millwork, and specialty items due to its softness and ease of working.
